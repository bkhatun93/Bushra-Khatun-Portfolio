Were we successful or not? Why or why not? Yes, the project was successful overall. The original goal was to develop a functional and professional portfolio website, and that goal was achieved. The website now presents a stronger structure, more complete content, improved usability, and a polished final appearance. Although some tasks required more time than expected, the project met its primary objectives and produced a live final deliverable. B. What were the milestones and subtasks? The main milestones included planning, prototype development, content refinement, usability improvement, and final completion. Subtasks included defining goals, organizing the site structure, writing case studies, revising content for clarity, testing responsiveness, updating navigation, and preparing the final report, Gantt chart, and presentation materials. C. Where did we miss deadlines, and how could that be prevented? A few content-related tasks extended beyond the original timeline because content writing, revision, and design refinement required more time than expected. A manager could prevent similar delays in the future by building buffer time into the schedule, breaking large tasks into smaller checkpoints, and conducting regular progress reviews to identify risks earlier. D. What did we learn from the past 8 weeks? This project reinforced the importance of realistic planning, iterative improvement, and strong attention to user experience. I learned that content development often takes more time than expected, that feedback and revision strengthen the final result, and that small visual details can significantly affect professionalism. E. How will completing this project impact the future? Completing this project strengthened my confidence in managing a project from concept to delivery. It improved my skills in project planning, communication, writing, design refinement, and professional presentation. These are valuable skills that will support my academic growth and future work in operations and project management. F.
